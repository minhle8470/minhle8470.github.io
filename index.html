Goals and Classroom Implementation
Curriculum

Enables students to develop a positive self-concept as learners of mathematics, obtain enjoyment from mathematics, and become self-motivated learners through inquiry and active participation in challenging and engaging experiences within the classroom.

  • Create lesson plans that based on NESA Syllabus, while challenging students to apply basic concepts to real-world contexts, as well as being inclusive of the learning needs of all diversive students.
  • Promote literacy, critical-thinking, problem solving, collaboration and applications to everyday life for all students.
Assessment

A wide variety of methods or tools that educators use to evaluate, measure, and document the academic readiness, learning progress, skill acquisition, or educational needs of students.

  • Summative Assessment: the use of scored based tasks such as; graded exams, assignments, projects which will determine whether students have learned what they were expected to retain during the defined instructional period.
  • Formative Assessment: in-process evaluations of student learning that are typically administered multiple times during a subject in which provides feedback for educator to understand what students do not grasp and needs further revision.
    Example: At the end of each mathematical topic, there will be a topic test/quiz based on what was done in previous lessons to ensure and modify our teaching approach which suits the need of the students.
  • Informal Assessment – continuous monitoring to evaluate student mathematical skills and modifying lesson plans to allow students to understand the content which is being taught that week.
    Example: There is a need to analyse the classroom to check if all students are able to comprehend what the class intentions of the lesson, if students are unable to understand is required re-establish the foundation of the lesson through a different method.
  • Student Formative Self-Assessment To allow self-evaluate of the students' strength and weakness upon the students' performance. This will also be able to recognise and be responsible for their own learning growth.
    Example: Support students to self-reflect on the lesson through a form, to dissect and allow students to consider on the area of mathematics that students need to work on. And will require students to hand back the form so educator can also can determine what is needed to improve our lesson plan.
